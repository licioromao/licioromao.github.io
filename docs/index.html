Research

Feedback control is a central concept in the study of dynamical systems, as it enables robustness against modelling and measurement errors. Such a concept has led to tremendeous advancements in several industries, e.g., aeronautical and aerospace, chemecial, biology, to name a few. Standard feedback design techniques, however, rely on mathematical models to the design optimal controllers, and thus cannot be applied to applications for which the underlying model is too complex or may not be obtained using physical principles.

My research combines techniques from formal verification, control theory, applied mathematics, and machine learning to enable the design of safer and more reliable feedback systems and to address theoretical and practical aspects of the decision-making process under uncertainty. More especifically, my research interests include:

  • Dynamic programming and Reinforcement learning
  • Abstraction of dynamical systems
  • Formal verification
  • Stochastic systems
  • Stochastic Optimization
  • Feedback control systems
